What the project does

The framework is designed to estimate survival-related clinical risk by training a model that learns from patient features while taking outcome timing and uncertainty into account. Instead of treating the problem as a simple binary prediction task, I approached it with survival-focused evaluation logic.

This helped the system better reflect the needs of real forecasting scenarios where risk estimation, calibration quality, and generalization matter as much as raw classification performance.

Build Details

The system was structured around sound modeling choices, careful validation, and clinically meaningful evaluation.

01

Modeling approach

Used LightGBM as the main model backbone for strong structured-data performance and flexible experimentation.

02

Censoring-aware weighting

Integrated IPCW weighting to handle survival-style data more responsibly and reduce misleading signal bias.

03

Validation flow

Applied cross-validation to compare behavior across splits and keep the framework focused on generalization.

04

Calibration and metrics

Used calibration checks and AUC-based evaluation so the output quality was judged on reliability, not just raw fit.
